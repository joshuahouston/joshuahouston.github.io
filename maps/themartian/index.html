A Tale of Mark Watney's Survival on Mars

Sol 6 found Mark Watney all alone on the red planet after a violent dust storm forced the astronauts of Ares 3 to abort their month-long mission. En route to the escape in the MAV (Mars ascent vehicle), an antenna from the communications set-up broke off and struck Watney, puncturing his suit and knocking him out of sight from the others. The biosensors of Watney's suit indicated to the other crew a loss of all vitals. They were forced to leave him behind rather than risk the entire crew to recover his body.

I Will Survive

Mark Watney was not ready to die on Mars, at least, not on Sol 6. At the Hab, he quickly got to work on a potato farm that would extend his food supply and set out to find a way to contact Earth.

It's Sirius

Mark Watney devised a set of rover experimental missions he named Sirius missions. He would have to test out the rovers, solar panels, and batteries to plan and prepare for a long drive. Sirius 1 led to his conclusion that he would need to direct as much solar energy into driving, and that means his heat would need to come from elsewhere.

"As with most of life's problems, this one can be solved by a box of pure radiation."

The radioactive thermoelectric generator radiated heat naturally and it would prove to be ample for Mark's needs. He found the RTG buried in a small hill marked by a green flag to serve as a warning, due south a NASA recommended 4 km away from the Hab. After Watney unearthed (unmarred?) the RTG, he declared Sirius 2 a success and went back to the Hab for a quick Sirius 3 and to plan Sirius 4.

Featureless Landscape

After one day's drive away from the Hab, the ancient river delta, Acidalia Planitia, provided Watney with no landmarks to navigate by. He would've considered making a compass but Mars has no magnetic field. His only option was to use one of Mars' two moons, Phobos, as a guide.

The Interplanetary Voyeur

Mindy Park kept daily (soly?) tabs on Watney's location ever since satellite images of deployed pop-tents and clean solar arrays tipped her off to his survival. Seeing his current location from the Hab as not just another driving test, she decided to alert Mars operations director, Venkat Kapoor. They charted his location and the Hab on a Mars map in a break room and pieced together the puzzle: he was headed for Pathfinder.

The Geology Nerd That She Is

Eventually, the wandering Watney reached the end of the delta and some hills came into view. With the hills to his right, he dubbed the valley ahead of him Lewis Valley, after the Ares 3 commander. If the King of Mars named it, not even NASA can argue.

God of Fear

Lewis Valley spit Mark back out into another featureless desert and once again he relied on the god of fear for guidance, that being Phobos; Watney was not amused.

Lighthouse of Alexandria

A small crater appeared before him, too small to be named, but still found on the map. With this beacon of hope he made his way towards Hamelin crater and beyond.

Pathfinder!

Mars is half the size of Earth and since it is also less dense, its gravitational pull is a mere 38% that of Earth's. Even with this advantage, the Mars Pathfinder Lander was not easy to load onto the rover. Ancient Egyptian techniques were used. On the other hand, like a good baby, the Sojourner Rover loaded easily into the cab.

Gone With The Wind

Mark Watney was able to follow his tracks back to Lewis Valley, but upon reaching Acidalia Planitia, found that all his former tracks were erased. He pressed on and eventually received a brief signal from the Hab. With 70's tv shows to strengthen his perserverance, he got close enough to maintain a signal from the Hab and followed it all the way home.

Hab Sweet Hab

Pathfinder allowed Watney to make contact with Earth but not everything was hunky dory from then on. Over the next few hundred sols, Watney encountered his share of Mars hardships, one of which led to a loss of contact with Earth. His survival came down to whether or not he could make it to the Ares 4 MAV in time to catch the Ares 3 crew fly-by in the Hermes spacecraft.

Schiaparelli or bust!

The Ares 4 MAV sat 3200 kilometers away from the Ares 3 Hab, as the crow flies. The same distance as Detroit from Los Angeles.

The Mars Canyon

Mark Watney navigated his way out of Acidalia Planitia just fine and into the canyon valley known as Mawrth Vallis. The canyon delivered on Mark's expectations of being an easy drive. Along the way and when convenient, he left messages in rocks to update NASA on his status and plans.

Between Two Craters

The traveler needed to pass through two craters Rutherford and Trouvelot. The system of using Phobos to roughly guess directions wasn't good enough this time around. He needed to be more accurate. Deneb, like Earth's Polaris, and a homemade sextant provided the latitude coordinates. A strict record-keeping of Phobos' setting time and a formula allowed for calculating his longitude.

Watney Triangle

As he passed through the two craters, Watney entered into an area bounded by craters in the shape of a triangle. He decided to name it the Watney Triangle. He aimed for the southeast corner of the triangle, Marth Crater.

No Way Around It

When the astronaut realized he had hit Marth Crater dead on, he took some time to guage which way around it would be better. Looking around the Martian landscape, he pieced together some things that had been puzzling him. The only explanation for his recently decreased solar cell output and the asymmetrical visibility was a dust storm. He was heading into a dust storm.

Martian Stormchaser

He needed to measure the storm. He left a solar panel and gauge at Marth crater, drove 40 km south left another one and drove another 40 and left another.

That's a lot of Potatoes!

At this point, Mark Watney was sick of potatoes and vowed to buy a home in Western Australia because it was on the opposite side of Earth from Idaho.

Mars Highway 1

The solar panel efficiency loggers worked. Mark had measured the storm and found it to be weaker farther south. Now he just had to drive the same path for the 3rd time.

Unexpected Opportunity

After going far south to get past the storm, Watney realized he wasn't far from the Opportunity rover which he could possibly use to contact Earth. The thought of it tempted him, but he pressed on. Schiaperelli Crater lie due east ahead of him.

Do A Barrel Roll

The Martian terrain deceived the hopeful driver on his descent into the welcoming crater. However, this was not Watney's first Martian rodeo and he was determined to not let this keep him from his goal. He fixed what damage was done and decided to drive slower the rest of the way.

"Then he knelt on one knee and fist-pumped repeatedly"

Mark made it to the MAV. A 3200 kilometer trek across Mars with gear that was only meant to last for a 31-day mission. Despite the impossible odds of this accomplishment, his toughest mission lay before him. The Ares 3 crew could not slow down to pick him up as would normally happen from the MAV. The MAV had to launch at such a speed as to catch up with the Hermes.

Bring Him Home

Mark Watney launched into space from the Martian surface after 549 sols on the planet. His rocketship was being controlled remotely, he had stripped it of all excess weight, all extra guidance systems, and all precautionary systems. The Hab canvas that covered the hole where a heavy hatch had been fluttered furiously in the thin Martian air. This drag slowed the rocket to a speed hopeless of interception from the Hermes. Fortunately, Watney's cleverness had spread to his crew. They blasted a hole in one of the ship's airlocks to get enough thrust to intercept Watney's MAV. With one of the crew on a tether, Watney was brought aboard.

The world rejoiced.
