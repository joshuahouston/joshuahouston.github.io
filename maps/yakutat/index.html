Portaging

Portaging was a grueling .7 miles of carrying the kayaks and all the gear. Several trips back and forth were made.

First night in Russell Fiord Wilderness

Cheese on cheese. We also lost the little thing to grab the pots and pans. That was a pain. We were all exhausted from the portage.

Cape Enchantment

We had a rough time with dinner since we lost the pot holder. I think I spilled boiling fresh water. It was sad. But Rachel's meal turned out to be wonderful despite my attempts to sabatoge dinner.

Glacier View

We heard the thunderous sounds of the glacier all night long. Each time a chunk broke off and splashed into the ocean, the waves surged back and forth like water in a giant bathtub.

Imax theater presents: Hubbard Glacier. Showing times: 24/7

We nicknamed parts of the glacier so we could tell each other where to look if we saw it calving. We could've spent all day there just watching the glacier calve. The excitement did not abate.

Wild Strawberries and Nagoon Berries

We had a gorgeous evening and nature provided dessert: wild strawberries. The nagoon berries were not quite ripe.

Nunatak and back

We paddled up and down the Nunatak Fiord, saw another bear, and had the wettest evening of the trip with no fire to keep us warm.

Brussel Sprouts

We made the most mileage, 18 miles I think. Then we ate a yummy brussel sprouts meal and went to bed anticipating the fun portage we'd be doing in the morning. We played a card game I think, too. And we dreamt of blueberry pancakes and greasy food waiting for us at Fat Grandma's
