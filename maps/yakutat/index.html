Kayaking Russell Fiord to Hubbard Glacier

A trip to Yakutat, Alaska to kayak up Russell Fiord to see the Hubbard Glacier. Yakutat's population is a mere 622 persons and dwindling. There is no cell-service and every driver waves at you as you're driving down the road, sometimes with both hands. Yakutat is known for its great surfing and the fast-moving glaciers, both retreating and advancing.

Portaging

Portaging was a grueling .7 miles of carrying the kayaks and all the gear. Several trips back and forth were made.

First night in Russell Fiord Wilderness

I made my special asparagus quesadillas, which were dubbed "cheese on cheese" for their pepper jack cheese and cream cheese combination. It was at this campsite that we left behind the pot-gripper to grab the pots and pans off of the fire. After asparagus quesadillas, we ate caramel apples. We were all exhausted from the portage and were relieved to crawl into our tents.

Cape Enchantment

We saw a sow and two cubs our second day in the wilderness. We also saw a dead baby porpoise. Making dinner proved to be more difficult without our pot holder. I spilled boiling fresh water, it was sad. But Rachel's meal turned out to be wonderful despite my attempts to sabotoge dinner. The noise from the calving glacier, like a distant storm, was an exciting tease for what was soon to come. Each time we hear a rumble, we asked Andy, "Did you hear that??"

Glacier View

Instead of a distant storm, this time the calving glacier was like a storm in your front yard. And the resulting surge of waves were nipping at the foot of our beds. Each time a chunk broke off and splashed into the ocean, the waves surged back and forth like water in a giant bathtub.

Imax theater presents: Hubbard Glacier. Showing times: 24/7

We nicknamed parts of the glacier so we could tell each other where to look if we saw it calving. We could've spent all day there just watching the glacier calve. The excitement did not abate.

Wild Strawberries and Nagoon Berries

We had a gorgeous evening and nature provided dessert: wild strawberries. The nagoon berries were not quite ripe.

Nunatak and back

We paddled up and down the Nunatak Fiord, saw another bear, and had the wettest evening of the trip with no fire to keep us warm.

Brussel Sprouts

We made the most mileage, 18 miles I think. Then we ate a yummy brussel sprouts meal and went to bed anticipating the fun portage we'd be doing in the morning. We played a card game I think, too. We dreamt of blueberry pancakes and greasy food waiting for us at Fat Grandma's.
